Computer History: the 70's

Early forms of networks were succesfully able to
connect to computer. But theres was a limit to how
big communites could grow. So they tried to create
the "networks of networks" a process called internetworking or internetting

The first advertisement for a microprocessor, the Intel 4004, appears
in Electronic News. Developed for a Japanese
calculator, it had the 2250 transistors
and could make up to 90000 calculations/minute.

Dennis Ritchie and his team created the "C"programming
language and released it to the public. It was
based on the earlier language BCPL (Basic Combined Programming Language)
and was a huge success.
